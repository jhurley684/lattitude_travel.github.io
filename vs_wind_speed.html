Lattidude vs. Wind Speed

Does the Latitude Affect The Wind Speed?


To some, wind speed is their primary issue. Kite flyers and sailors like some wind but not a lot. In some hot places a light breeze is refreshing. But big gusts of wind can be an endless nusance to picnickers, sportsment, and others.

Whatever your preference, can you get the right amount of wind-speed just by heading to the right latitude? The chart to the left says it's doubtful - here's why:

As you can see data points cluster toward the bottom of the plot. That is because wind speed are generally light. And, of course, wind speed can't go below zero. Low wind speeds are evenly distrubuted along all lattitudes - everywhere has at least some wind.

But what about high winds? According to our wind speed plot, high winds tend a bit more often at very high latitudes but unless your a penguin or a polar bear you aren't anymore likely to run into high winds regardless of your latitude. So for all practical reasons, latitude doesn't make any difference to your traveling needs.
